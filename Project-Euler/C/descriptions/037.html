Truncatable Primes

Problem 37

The number of 3797 has an interesting property. Being prime itself, it is possible to continuously remove digits from the left to right, and remain prime at each stage: 3797, 797, 97, and 7. Similarly we can work from right to left: 3797, 379, 37, 3.

Find the sum of the only eleven primes that are both truncatable from left to right and right to left.

NOTE: 2, 3, 5, and 7 are not considered to be truncatable primes.

Answer: 748,317
